1. Introduction  
Electronic commerce (E -commerce) apps are pla tforms that is used so the user 
can buy and sell products [ 1], the rise of e -commerce and web apps has made 
recommender systems a key part of our daily lives, suggesting personalized items 
based on user preferences [2], Traditional systems, are limited by the data they have 
available, typically just a user's browsing history or past purchases. This restricts 
them to recommending products that are similar to what the user has already 
interacted with [3], and it is also may not universally cater to all users, and in some 
cases, could evoke resistance, creating a sense of aversion among users [4] 
Large language models (LLMs) like ChatGPT and GPT -4 are further 
revolutionizing NLP and AI with their ability to underst and and generate human 
language [2], this LLMs received considerable attention due to their capabilities and 
their great achievements in many fields [5], they are also impacting online shopping, 
as customers rely more on LLMs for quick and informed purchase decisions [3]. 
Traditional recommender systems focus on analyzing user behavior data, but 
combining LLM with recommender systems can become revolutionary, the 
combination can help in understanding explicit feedback from users, and understand 
their needs a nd preferences, it will also help in improving the accuracy and 
satisfaction of recommender systems [6]. 
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are highly effective in tasks like 
visual question answering and understanding.  The success of Large Language 
Models has led to efforts to combine them with other modalities for improved 
performance, as seen in models like GPT-4V and Gemini [7]. 
Augmented Reality (AR) is playing a significant role in the evolution of online 
shopping. Retailers are increasingly ado pting AR to enhance the virtual shopping 
experience, making it more fun, effective, and enjoyable. Brands like Timberland, 4  
  
Sephora, and Topshop have integrated AR into their mobile apps, allowing 
consumers to virtually try on products  [8], it also improves sensory experiences by 
adding virtual elements into real time environment [15] 
Visuals and text are important for decision -making, highlighting the need for 
systems that integrate various data streams for better personalization.  Users often 
need different  information systems, like e -commerce platforms with search and 
recommendation features, to improve their experience [16] 
This research combines physical and digital shopping, offering personalized 
product suggestions and AR experiences to enhance decision -making and support 
retailer growth, it also explores  the technical foundation, user acceptance, and 
potential industry impact of this novel approach. 
2. Research Problem 
E-commerce platforms have traditionally relied on conventional text-based search 
methods, making it challenging for users to find products that match their visual 
preferences or complex requirements. While some platforms offer visual search or 
AR previews independently, there is a significant gap in integrating these 
technologies into a un ified, intuitive shopping experience. The lack of seamless 
integration between natural language understanding, visual search, and AR 
visualization often leads to fragmented user experiences and reduced shopping 
effectiveness. 
This research addresses this l imitation by proposing an innovative unified 
architecture that combines multimodal language models, visual search capabilities, 
and AR technology.  
The proposed solution aims to create a more intuitive and immersive e-commerce 
experience through: 5  
  
1. Integration of multimodal language models to process and understand both 
text and image inputs, enabling more natural and context -aware product 
search  
2. Implementation of advanced visual search capabilities that allow users to 
find products based on visual similarities and attributes  
3. Incorporation of AR technology for real -time product visualization, 
enabling users to preview products in their intended environment  
4. Development of a seamless workflow from search to purchase, enhancing 
the overall shopping experience 
The significance of this research lies in: 
 Creating a more natural and intuitive shopping experience that aligns with 
how users naturally think about and search for products 
 Reducing the gap between online product discovery and real -world 
visualization 
 Improving purchase confidence through immersive product previews 
 Enhancing user satisfaction by providing multiple input modalities for 
product search 
 
3. Research Objectives 
This research aims to enhance the user experience and also contributes 
valuable insights to the fields AI, and e-commerce by:  
• Improve user experience: The primary objective of the research is to improve 
the overall user experience in an e -commerce application by integrating visual 
search, image generation, and augmented reality technologies. This will enable 
users to easily search for products, visualize them in their environment, and make 
informed purchasing decisions. 6  
  
• Increase conversion rates: The research aims to increase conversion rates in an 
e-commerce application by guiding users through the checkout flow and making 
it easier for them to add items to their cart.  
• Improving accuracy and relevance: The research aims to improve the accuracy 
and relevance of product recommendations and search results by using visual 
search, image generation, and augmented reality technologies. 
• Elevate user satisfaction: The research aims to evaluate user satisfaction with 
the integrated visual search, image generation, and augmented reality 
technologies in an e-commerce application.

moving beyond traditional single-modal interaction models and proposing new 
frameworks for understanding multimodal user engagement.  It explores the 
relationship between visual and textual information processing in e -7

6. Literature Review 
(Y. Jin et al., 2023) the paper presents a novel multi-modal foundation 
model called ECLIP, designed for large -scale applications in E -commerce. 
Recognizing the limitations of existing vision-language pre-training methods 
when applied to E-commerce due to the distinct nature of product images, the 
authors propose an instance-centric pre-training paradigm. ECLIP features a 
decoder architecture that utilizes learnable instance queries to capture 
instance-level semantics effectively. Additionally, it introduces two specially 
designed pretext tasks that allow the model to focus on specific product 
instances without the need for costly manual annotations. Trained on a dataset 
of 100 million E-commerce-related items, ECLIP demonstrates the ability to 8  
  
extract more generic, semantic-rich, and robust representations. Experimental 
results indicate that ECLIP significantly outperforms existing methods across 
various downstream tasks, showcasing its strong transferability to real -world 
E-commerce applications. Overall, the framework emphasizes learning 
instance-level representations through innovative architecture and pre-training 
tasks, highlighting its superior generalization capabilities [9] 
(B. Xu et al., 2024) this paper introduces Mind, a framework enhancing 
purchase intention understanding in E -commerce. Mind uses Large Vision-
Language Models to infer intentions from product metadata and human 
insights, creating a database of 1.  2 million intentions.  Human evaluations 
confirm validity and usefulness, showing improved intention 
comprehension. Mind automates intention gener ation and filtering, proving 
superior to previous methods. This approach advances E-commerce intention 
acquisition and application, benefiting student models in comprehension tasks 
[10]. 
(Z. Hu et al., 2024) introduces Multi-Modal Learning with online Token 
Pruning (MML-TP) as a new method to improve vision-language transformer 
models for e -commerce product searches.  It addresses the challenge of 
aligning non -visual descriptive text attributes in product descriptions with 
visual data.  MML-TP uses token pruning to clean text during training, 
enhancing the paired image branch.  Testing on 710,000 Amazon products 
showed that refining text tokens boosts visual search performance.  This 
approach enhances image-text alignment, strengthening the vision encoder for 
more efficient e-commerce models compatible with various existing models  
[11] 9  
  
(H. Chen, et al., 2024) this paper introduces the Visual Construction 
Safety Query (VCSQ) system, aiming to boost situational awareness in 
construction sites to prevent acciden ts. VCSQ combines Image Captioning 
(IC), Visual Question Answering (VQA), and Image -Text Retrieval (ITR) 
using Augmented Reality (AR) headsets. Results showed high recall rates for 
ITR, 89. 7% accuracy for VQA, and 0.  449 SPICE score for IC.  Feasibility 
tests confirmed practical benefits, especially for safety in construction.  The 
system integrates IC, VQA, and ITR to create an interactive interface for 
workers and management to improve safety and efficiency.  Future 
improvements include refining responses w ith a prompt -tuning strategy, 
integrating safety regulations, and focusing on user adoption and training 
modules [12]. 
(J. Bi, et al., 2023) discusses combining large language models (LLMs) 
with augmented reality (AR) to enhance human -computer interaction by 
integrating visual, auditory, and linguistic elements.  By incorporating LLMs 
into AR, task performance can be quantified more effectively, improving state 
estimation and creating more adaptive AR systems. Experimental results show 
promise for advancing AR applications with this integrated approach, 
particularly in improving video caption quality and text -based scene 
understanding for users [13] 
(D. Stover, 2024) this  paper introduces a new method for augmented 
reality task guidance systems, addressing the shortcomings of current systems 
that rely on expert input or are limited to specific tasks and environments. The 
proposed approach allows operators to take photos of objects and write task 
instructions in natural language, which the system converts into guidance 
visuals. The system, TAGGAR, uses machine learning models for 
understanding language and locating objects.  A user study showed that 10  
  
operators could easily create clear instructions, and end users could follow the 
guidance visuals accurately 85.  7% of the time, even without prior 
knowledge. TAGGAR provides a versatile solution for generating guidance 
in various settings, surpassing current limitations in AR systems [14] 
(J. Ji, et al., 2023) This paper proposes a method for building 
recommender systems that leverage Large Language Models (LLMs) to 
generate recommendations directly as textual descriptions, by collecting user-
product interaction data like purchase history, browsing behavior, and 
potentially product reviews, GenRec utilizes a pre-trained LLM, potentially a 
model like BERT or GPT-3, for recommendation generation, this LLM is fine-
tuned on the prepared user -product data. This helps the LLM understand the 
relationships between user preferences, product features, and the language 
used to describe them [3] 
(T. Wei, et al., 2024) The paper presents a Unified paradigm for Multi-
modal Personalization systems (UniMP) that combines different data types 
like images and text to improve user experience in tasks like recommendation 
and search, it u ses a flexible generative framework to analyze multi -modal 
user history for precise predictions and customized outputs, it features a 
thorough benchmark for assessing multi-modal personalized tasks, confirming 
its effectiveness for various user needs [16]. 
(Z. Li, et al., 2024) this paper presents UnifiedMLLM, a multi -modal 
large language model designed to unify the handling of diverse tasks across 
multiple modalities. UnifiedMLLM introduces task tokens and grounding 
tokens to represent task types and regio ns, enabling seamless integration of 
tasks through a task router and expert models. The system combines image, 
video, and audio encoders with a shared embedding space, supported by a 11  
  
three-stage training strategy to enhance perception, reasoning, and task 
execution. Experimental results demonstrated strong generalization and 
scalability, with superior performance across benchmarks such as image 
segmentation, reasoning -based editing, and multi -modal generation tasks. 
Future work focuses on end -to-end system design and improvements for 
handling interleaved multi-modal inputs [17] 
 (S. Hewawalpita, & I. Perera, 2019) presents a multimodal interaction 
framework for e -commerce that integrates three distinct interaction modes: 
speech inputs, interactive 3D visuali zations, and gesture controls. The 
framework implements speech recognition through Web Speech API for 
voice-based product searching, while 3D product presentations are created 
using multiple -angle product photographs to enable 360 -degree viewing. 
Gesture interactions are supported through both webcam -based recognition 
and Leap Motion device tracking. Built on the CodeIgniter MVC architecture, 
the framework separates server -side e-commerce functionalities from client -
side multimodal interactions, enabling se amless integration through 
interaction handlers and e -commerce context management. The system was 
evaluated through user studies, demonstrating improved product engagement 
and user experience, though revealing some cultural and technical challenges 
in multimodal adoption. Future work focuses on virtual reality integration and 
accessibility improvements [18]. 
(J. Chen et al., 2023) this paper presents MiniGPT-v2, a novel vision-
language model designed for efficient and effective multi -modal 
understanding. MiniGPT-v2 leverages a streamlined architecture comprising 
a Vision Transformer (ViT) encoder, a linear projection layer, and the 
powerful LLaMA -2 language model. By efficiently processing high -
resolution images (448x448) and mapping visual features into the  language 12  
  
model's space, MiniGPT -v2 excels in various vision -language tasks, 
MiniGPT-v2's training follows a three -stage approach. Initial pretraining 
utilizes a diverse mix of weakly-labeled and fine-grained image-text datasets. 
Subsequent multi -task fine -tuning refines performance on specific tasks. 
Finally, instruction tuning incorporates multi -modal instruction datasets and 
language-only datasets to enhance generalization and robustness, MiniGPT -
v2 demonstrates strong performance across a range of visio n-language 
benchmarks, including visual question answering, referring expression 
comprehension, and grounded image captioning [19]

7.1.2. System Architecture Overview: 13  
  
 
The MULTIVIEW Unified architecture consists of four primary layers 
that work together to create a seamless e-commerce experience: 
1. User Input Layer  
 Supports multiple input modalities:  
o Text queries for traditional keyword-based search 
o Image queries for visual product matching 
o Audio queries for voice-based interactions 
 Enables natural and flexible user interaction methods 
 14  
  
2. Multimodal Processing Layer  
 Comprises sophisticated AI components:  
o LLM Model for processing textual inputs 
o Vision Model for image analysis 
o Speech-to-Text processing for audio conversion 
o Unified Understanding Engine that integrates all inputs 
 Creates a cohesive understanding of user intent across modalities 
 
3. AR Visualization Layer  
 Enables users to visualize products in their real environment 
 
4. Checkout Layer  
 Streamlines the purchase process through:  
o Efficient Payment Processing 
o Immediate Order Confirmation 
 Ensures a smooth transition from product discovery to purchase 
This unified architecture addresses the core research objectives by: 
 Facilitating natural multimodal interaction 
 Enabling seamless integration of physical and digital shopping experiences 
 Supporting real-time visualization of products 
 Streamlining the purchase process 
7.1.3. Data Collection and Management: 
 Gather multimodal training data including  (product images with detailed 
descriptions - image-text paired data - visual attribute annotations - Product 
category hierarchies). 15  
  
 Implement multimodal preprocessing pipeline  for ( image preprocessing 
for model compatibility  - text tokenization and encoding  - data 
augmentation techniques). 
 
7.2. Multimodal Model Selection and Integration: 
7.2.1. Multimodal Model Implementation: 
 Select and implement base multimodal model  like: C LIP for visual -
semantic embeddings , LLaVA for detailed visual understanding and 
CogVLM for complex visual reasoning, etc. 
 Design model pipeline includes input handling for images or text, cross -
modal feature extraction, multimodal fusion strategy, and output 
generation.  
7.2.2. Visual Search Integration: 
 Create visual search using multiple features, including unified 
embeddings, incorporate similarity computation, efficient indexing, and a 
ranking algorithm in the design. 
 Develop a hybrid search system that integrates text and visual queries, the 
system should give importance to various types of information and be able 
to handle queries that consist of multiple images.  
7.2.3. AR Visualization Integration: 
 Implement AR visualization and connect it with the multimodal system
